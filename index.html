03. Advanced Techniques

Reasoning architectures that simulate human cognition. Tap each card to expand.

Zero-Shot

Direct Instruction

−

No examples provided. Best for simple, clear requests.

"Write a syllabus note about BYUI AI use policy for students."
Best for: Quick document generation, policy summaries
Vague or unstructured requests frequently result in outputs that are generic, contextually inappropriate, or factually inaccurate — zero-shot works only when the task is straightforward or you need a rough draft to start.
Few-Shot

Exemplar Learning

+

Provide 1–3 examples to match institutional tone.

"Here are 3 empathetic warning emails we've used. Draft a new one in this same voice for a student on academic probation."
Best for: Consistent tone in outreach, matching an advisor's voice
Research shows even 1–3 examples significantly improves the model's ability to match desired format and vocabulary for differentiated student levels.
Chain-of-Thought

Financial Aid Appeal

+

Reason through a student's circumstances step-by-step before drafting an appeal — so every claim is grounded in logic an advisor can verify.

"A student lost financial aid after falling below the GPA requirement. Their situation: family medical emergency in Week 10, withdrew from two courses late, GPA dropped from 3.1 to 1.8. Step 1: Identify which appeal criteria this student likely meets. Step 2: Rank the strength of each argument. Step 3: Identify any gaps or risks in the appeal. Step 4: Draft a 200-word appeal letter using only the strongest argument. Show your reasoning before each step."
Best for: Financial aid appeals, SAP disputes, exception requests
By requiring the AI to reason through eligibility before drafting, advisors can catch faulty logic — e.g., if the AI wrongly assumes a medical emergency auto-qualifies — before the letter reaches the financial aid office. The reasoning trail is the safeguard.
Analogical Prompting

Problem Recall

+

Ask the AI to recall similar problems before solving the current one.

"What should a student do if they failed a core course that is only offered in the fall? Recall similar scheduling dilemmas before answering."
Best for: Complex edge cases, non-standard student situations
Improved problem-solving for complex scenarios that don't fit standard policy language.

04. Empathy & NVC Framework

Nonviolent Communication (OFNR) — keeping outreach human-centered. Tap each component.

Prompt Example
"Describe the student's three missed assignments without using evaluative language."
Student Success Outcome
Avoids making the student feel judged or defensive, keeping the door open for support.

Affective Computing & Role-Play Training

Use AI for "do-over" role-play simulations for staff training. Advisors can simulate contentious meetings or difficult financial aid conversations — practicing empathy-driven responses in a safe environment. Advanced multimodal AI can even analyze backchannel cues like breathing rates or facial expressions.

"Simulate a meeting where a student is frustrated about being placed on academic probation. I will play the advisor. Respond as the student and give me feedback on my empathy and de-escalation after each exchange."
