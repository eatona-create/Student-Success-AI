# AI for Student Success

## Overview

This repository documents ongoing work at **Brigham Young University–Idaho (BYU-Idaho)** focused on applying artificial intelligence (AI) to improve student success outcomes. The work is grounded in the university’s distinctive mission and Learning Model, with a particular emphasis on academic advising, student support services, and institutional effectiveness.

At BYU-Idaho, student success is closely tied to principles such as student-centered learning, instructor-guided engagement, and meaningful student preparation. This initiative explores how AI can responsibly augment these principles by enhancing human decision-making, expanding access to support, and enabling more personalized, scalable student experiences without compromising the university’s values.

The effort is not purely technical; it is institutional. It seeks to align AI capabilities with BYU-Idaho’s commitment to developing disciples of Jesus Christ who are leaders in their homes, the Church, and their communities, ensuring that technology serves—not replaces—the human elements central to that mission.

---

## Objectives

* Improve student retention, persistence, and completion rates
* Enhance the quality and consistency of academic advising
* Increase access to timely, accurate student support
* Reduce administrative burden on staff through automation
* Enable data-informed decision-making at scale

---

## Key Focus Areas

### 1. AI in Academic Advising

* Intelligent advising assistants to support common student inquiries
* Degree planning and pathway optimization
* Early alert systems for at-risk students
* Advisor augmentation (not replacement)

### 2. Student Support Automation

* AI-powered chat and self-service tools
* Knowledge base integration and retrieval
* Case triage and routing
* 24/7 support availability

### 3. Data & Predictive Analytics

* Risk modeling for retention and academic performance
* Behavioral and engagement signal analysis
* Intervention timing optimization

### 4. Personalization

* Adaptive recommendations for courses and resources
* Context-aware student guidance
* Tailored communication strategies

### 5. Ethical & Responsible AI

* Data privacy and FERPA compliance
* Bias detection and mitigation
* Transparency and explainability
* Human-in-the-loop governance

---

## Guiding Principles

* **Student-Centered**: Prioritize student outcomes and experience in alignment with BYU-Idaho’s Learning Model
* **Augmentation Over Replacement**: Support advisors, faculty, and staff—preserving high-touch mentoring relationships
* **Responsible AI**: Ensure ethical, transparent, FERPA-compliant use of student data
* **Scalability**: Design solutions that can expand across campuses, programs, and modalities (on-campus and online)
* **Alignment with Mission**: Ensure all AI applications reinforce BYU-Idaho’s mission and spiritual, educational, and developmental objectives
